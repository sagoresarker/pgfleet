Why self-host

The managed-Postgres experience, without the managed-Postgres bill.

PgFleet gives small teams and solo operators the operational guardrails of a cloud database service while keeping the data, the bucket, and the control plane entirely under your roof.

Own the whole stack

Postgres runs in Docker on your host; backups land in your S3 or MinIO bucket. No data leaves your environment and there is no per-instance cloud premium.

Disaster recovery built in

Continuous WAL archiving plus scheduled full and differential backups via pgBackRest, with a guided point-in-time recovery wizard for when things go wrong.

Operate with confidence

Live and historical analytics, query insights, a SQL console, alerting, and in-house automatic failover keep your fleet observable and resilient.

How it works

From docker compose up to a recoverable fleet in four moves.

STEP 01

Provision

Spin up a Postgres instance in Docker — choose version, extensions like TimescaleDB, and resources — straight from the dashboard.

STEP 02

Archive WAL

The instant it is live, write-ahead logs stream to your object store. Every committed transaction is durable off-host within seconds.

STEP 03

Backup & PITR

Scheduled full and differential backups via pgBackRest. Recover to any second in your retention window with a guided wizard.

STEP 04

Observe

Watch live throughput, replication lag, slow queries, and logs. Set alerts and failover so the fleet looks after itself.

Live operations console

One pane of glass for the whole fleet.

Health, throughput, replication, and backup state for every instance — rendered live in the dashboard. No tab-juggling between psql, docker ps, and your backup logs.
